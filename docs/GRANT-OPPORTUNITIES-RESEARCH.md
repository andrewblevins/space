## Overview

This document identifies potential grant funding sources for SPACE Terminal as a research tool focused on cognitive enhancement, AI ethics, human-AI collaboration, and multi-perspective thinking.

## Federal Grant Opportunities

### National Science Foundation (NSF)

#### 1. **Human-Centered Computing (HCC) Program**
- **Program**: CISE/IIS - Human-Centered Computing
- **Focus**: Technologies that enhance human capabilities, cognitive tools, human-AI interaction
- **Funding**: $500K-$1.5M over 3-5 years
- **Fit**: Strong - SPACE directly addresses human-centered AI design
- **Key Points**:
  - Multi-perspective AI conversations align with "enhancing human cognition"
  - User sovereignty and transparent alignment are core to human-centered design
  - Evaluation system demonstrates measurable cognitive benefits
- **Application Type**: Standard Research Grant
- **Deadlines**: Multiple deadlines per year
- **Link**: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/pgm_summ.jsp?pims_id=503325

#### 2. **Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency in AI (FAT-AI)**
- **Program**: CISE/IIS - Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency
- **Focus**: AI systems that are transparent, accountable, and fair
- **Funding**: $500K-$1M over 3-4 years
- **Fit**: Very Strong - SPACE's core value is transparency and user sovereignty
- **Key Points**:
  - Full prompt visibility addresses transparency
  - User-defined optimization addresses accountability
  - Multi-perspective design addresses bias reduction
- **Application Type**: Research Grant
- **Deadlines**: Annual deadlines
- **Link**: https://www.nsf.gov/funding/pgm_summ.jsp?pims_id=505789

#### 3. **Cyber-Human Systems (CHS)**
- **Program**: CISE/IIS - Cyber-Human Systems
- **Focus**: Seamless integration of human and computational intelligence
- **Funding**: $500K-$2M over 3-5 years
- **Fit**: Strong - SPACE is a cyber-human system
- **Key Points**:
  - Multi-perspective conversations demonstrate human-AI collaboration
  - Evaluation system shows iterative human-AI co-creation
  - Cognitive capacity building is measurable outcome
- **Application Type**: Standard Research Grant
- **Deadlines**: Multiple deadlines per year

#### 4. **Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR)**
- **Program**: NSF SBIR Phase I/II
- **Focus**: Commercializable research with societal impact
- **Funding**: Phase I: $256K, Phase II: $750K-$1.6M
- **Fit**: Moderate - Requires commercialization plan
- **Key Points**:
  - SPACE has clear commercial potential (SaaS model)
  - Research validation strengthens commercial case
  - Open source approach aligns with public benefit
- **Application Type**: SBIR Grant
- **Deadlines**: Quarterly deadlines
- **Link**: https://seedfund.nsf.gov/

### National Institutes of Health (NIH)

#### 5. **Cognitive Enhancement and Decision-Making**
- **Program**: NIMH/NIA - Cognitive Enhancement Research
- **Focus**: Tools that improve cognitive function and decision-making
- **Funding**: $200K-$500K over 2-3 years
- **Fit**: Moderate - Requires health/clinical angle
- **Key Points**:
  - Multi-perspective thinking improves decision quality
  - Could position as tool for reducing cognitive biases
  - Potential applications in mental health (perspective-taking therapy)
- **Application Type**: R01 or R21 Research Grant
- **Deadlines**: Multiple deadlines per year
- **Note**: May require partnership with health researcher

## Grant Application Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation Grants (Months 1-6)
**Target**: Open Philanthropy, Mozilla Foundation
- **Why**: Less formal process, faster decisions, aligned values
- **Timeline**: 2-3 months from inquiry to decision
- **Amount**: $50K-$200K
- **Use**: Cover infrastructure costs, validate research direction

### Phase 2: Academic Partnerships (Months 3-12)
**Target**: MIT Media Lab, Stanford HAI, CMU HCII
- **Why**: Research validation, credibility, access to larger grants
- **Timeline**: 3-6 months to establish partnership
- **Benefit**: Joint NSF/NIH grant applications

### Phase 3: Federal Grants (Months 6-18)
**Target**: NSF HCC, NSF FAT-AI, NSF SBIR
- **Why**: Larger funding, multi-year support, research validation
- **Timeline**: 6-12 months from application to decision
- **Amount**: $500K-$1.5M over 3-5 years

## Grant Application Components

### 1. **Research Questions**
- Does multi-perspective AI conversation improve decision quality?
- Does transparent alignment increase user trust and engagement?
- Does user-defined optimization lead to better outcomes than metric-driven optimization?
- Can multi-perspective thinking be taught/learned through tool use?
- Does opponent processing reduce cognitive biases?

### 2. **Research Methodology**
- **User Studies**: Longitudinal studies of SPACE users
- **Controlled Experiments**: SPACE vs. single-perspective AI
- **Cognitive Assessments**: Before/after thinking quality measures
- **Qualitative Research**: User interviews, case studies

### 3. **Expected Outcomes**
- **Publications**: 2-3 peer-reviewed papers per year
- **Open Source Tool**: Maintained and improved
- **Research Data**: Anonymized datasets for research community
- **Best Practices**: Guidelines for user-sovereign AI design

### 4. **Budget Breakdown** (Example: $200K over 2 years)
- **Infrastructure**: $20K/year (API costs, hosting)
- **Research Support**: $50K/year (research assistant, data analysis)
- **Development**: $30K/year (part-time development)
- **Dissemination**: $10K/year (conferences, publications)
- **Indirect Costs**: $20K/year (university overhead if partnered)
