# SaaS Pricing Validation Survey

*Template for validating $15-20/month pricing through direct customer conversations*

---

## Survey Overview

**Purpose**: Validate willingness to pay $15-20/month for SPACE Terminal premium features

**Target**: 20-30 potential customers across different segments

**Method**: 15-30 minute conversations (phone/video) + optional follow-up survey

**Timeline**: 2-4 weeks to complete all conversations

---

## Target Segments

### Segment 1: Knowledge Workers (10 conversations)
- Consultants, researchers, analysts, writers, strategists
- **Recruitment**: LinkedIn, Twitter, professional communities
- **Key Question**: Would they pay $15-20/month for better decision-making tools?

### Segment 2: Executives & Leaders (5 conversations)
- Founders, executives, managers, board members
- **Recruitment**: Personal network, startup communities
- **Key Question**: Would they pay $20/month to pressure-test critical decisions?

### Segment 3: Academics & Researchers (5 conversations)
- Graduate students, postdocs, professors, independent scholars
- **Recruitment**: Academic Twitter, university communities
- **Key Question**: Would they pay $5-15/month (student/faculty pricing) for research tool?

### Segment 4: Creative Professionals (5 conversations)
- Writers, designers, artists, musicians, directors
- **Recruitment**: Creative communities, Twitter
- **Key Question**: Would they pay $15/month to break through creative blocks?

### Segment 5: Current SPACE Users (5 conversations)
- People already using SPACE Terminal (if available)
- **Recruitment**: Existing user base
- **Key Question**: What would make them pay? What's missing?

---

## Conversation Script

### Opening (2 minutes)

**Introduction**:
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time. I'm building SPACE Terminal, a tool for multi-perspective AI conversations. I'm talking to people like you to understand if there's a market for premium features at $15-20/month. This is a research conversation, not a sales pitch - I want your honest feedback."

**Context Setting**:
"SPACE lets you have conversations with multiple AI advisors simultaneously, each with different perspectives. You can create advisors representing different frameworks, stakeholders, or ways of thinking. The idea is to get multiple perspectives on complex problems rather than just one AI voice."

**Ask Permission**:
"Is it okay if I ask you some questions about your work and decision-making process? This should take about 15-20 minutes."

---

### Questions (15-20 minutes)

#### 1. Current Work & Decision-Making (5 minutes)

**Q1**: "Tell me about your work. What kinds of decisions or problems do you face regularly?"

**Follow-ups**:
- How do you currently approach complex decisions?
- Do you use any tools or frameworks?
- Have you tried AI tools like ChatGPT for decision-making?

**What to Listen For**:
- Complexity of decisions
- Current pain points
- Tool usage patterns
- Willingness to try new tools

---

#### 2. SPACE Demo & Value Prop (5 minutes)

**Show SPACE** (if possible) or describe:
- Multi-perspective conversations
- Advisor creation and management
- Evaluation system
- Expert knowledge advisors (premium feature)
- Advanced analytics (premium feature)

**Q2**: "What do you think? Does this sound useful for your work?"

**Follow-ups**:
- Which features interest you most?
- What concerns do you have?
- What's missing?

**What to Listen For**:
- Genuine interest vs. politeness
- Which features resonate
- Objections or concerns
- Use case fit

---

#### 3. Pricing Discussion (5 minutes)

**Q3**: "I'm considering pricing at $15-20/month for premium features. What's your reaction to that price?"

**Follow-ups**:
- Too high? Too low? Just right?
- What would make it worth that price?
- What's your comparison point? (ChatGPT Plus, Notion, etc.)
- Would you pay annually at a discount? ($150-180/year)

**What to Listen For**:
- Immediate reaction (facial expression, tone)
- Comparison points they mention
- What would justify the price
- Price sensitivity

---

#### 4. Willingness to Pay (5 minutes)

**Q4**: "If SPACE had these premium features - expert knowledge advisors, advanced analytics, 10x message limits - would you pay $15-20/month?"

**Follow-ups**:
- What would need to be true for you to pay?
- What would prevent you from paying?
- Would you try a free trial first?
- What if it was $10/month? $25/month?

**What to Listen For**:
- Clear yes/no/maybe
- Conditions for paying
- Deal-breakers
- Price elasticity

---

#### 5. Competitive Context (3 minutes)

**Q5**: "What other tools do you pay for? How much?"

**Follow-ups**:
- ChatGPT Plus? Notion? Roam? Other AI tools?
- What makes those worth paying for?
- How does SPACE compare?

**What to Listen For**:
- Current spending on tools
- What justifies their spending
- Competitive positioning

---

#### 6. Closing Questions (2 minutes)

**Q6**: "Anything else you want to share? Questions for me?"

**Q7**: "If I launched this, would you be interested in trying it? Can I follow up?"

**What to Listen For**:
- Additional insights
- Interest level
- Permission to follow up

---

### Closing (1 minute)

**Thank You**:
"Thank you so much for your time and honesty. This is really helpful. I'll keep you updated if we launch premium features."

**Next Steps**:
- Send follow-up survey (optional)
- Add to potential beta tester list
- Follow up when launching

   - [ ] Monthly ($15-20/month)

6. **What would prevent you from paying?** (Open text)

7. **How does SPACE compare to ChatGPT Plus ($20/month)?**
   - [ ] SPACE is worth more
   - [ ] About the same value
   - [ ] ChatGPT Plus is worth more

8. **Would you be interested in a free trial?**
   - [ ] Yes, 7 days

9. **Any other feedback?** (Open text)

## Analysis Framework

### After All Conversations

#### 1. Quantitative Analysis
- **Average Willingness to Pay** (1-10 scale)
- **Price Sensitivity**: How many said "too high" vs "just right" vs "too low"
- **Feature Prioritization**: Which features matter most
- **Segment Differences**: Do different segments have different price sensitivity?

#### 2. Qualitative Analysis
- **Common Themes**: What patterns emerge?
- **Objections**: What are the main concerns?
- **Use Cases**: What problems are people trying to solve?
- **Competitive Positioning**: How do they compare SPACE to alternatives?

#### 3. Decision Framework

**Green Light** (Proceed with $15-20/month):
- Average willingness to pay ≥ 7/10
- ≥60% say price is "just right" or "too low"
- Clear value proposition emerges
- Multiple segments show interest

**Yellow Light** (Proceed with Caution):
- Average willingness to pay 5-6/10
- 40-60% say price is "just right"
- Some interest but concerns exist
- May need to adjust pricing or features

**Red Light** (Reconsider):
- Average willingness to pay ≤ 4/10
- <40% say price is "just right"
- Major objections emerge
- Need to pivot pricing or value prop

## Recruitment Strategies

### 1. Personal Network
- Reach out to people you know who fit target segments
- Ask for introductions to others
- Leverage existing relationships

### 2. Social Media
- Twitter/X: Post about SPACE, ask for feedback
- LinkedIn: Reach out to professionals in target segments
- Reddit: Post in relevant communities (r/consulting, r/startups, etc.)

### 3. Communities
- Professional communities (Indie Hackers, Product Hunt)
- Academic communities (Twitter academic circles)
- Creative communities (writing groups, design communities)

### 4. Current Users
- If SPACE has users, reach out directly
- Offer early access to premium features
- Get feedback from people already using the tool

## Expected Outcomes

### Best Case
- Strong willingness to pay ($15-20/month validated)
- Clear value proposition
- Multiple segments interested
- Ready to launch premium tier

### Realistic Case
- Moderate willingness to pay
- Some price sensitivity
- Need to adjust features or pricing
- Iterate based on feedback

### Worst Case
- Low willingness to pay
- Major objections
- Need to pivot pricing model
- Consider alternative models (grant-funded, consulting, etc.)

## Next Steps After Validation

### If Pricing Validated:
1. Build premium features
2. Set up payment processing
3. Launch premium tier
4. Continue customer development

### If Pricing Not Validated:

2. Adjust pricing or value proposition
3. Re-validate with adjustments
4. Consider alternative models
